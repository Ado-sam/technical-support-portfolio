## **Clarifying Website Traffic Patterns to Address Security Concerns**

#### **Situation**

A customer became concerned that their website had been hacked after noticing more than 10,000 visits from China in their WordPress.com Stats. They interpreted the unexpected traffic as evidence of a potential security incident.

#### **Task**

Determine whether the reported traffic indicated a website compromise, explain the nature of the observed traffic, and provide guidance that would help the customer accurately interpret future traffic patterns.

#### **Actions**

* Reviewed the reported traffic patterns in WordPress.com Stats.
* Distinguished automated crawler activity from indicators of malicious website behavior.
* Explained how search engines and other automated services routinely crawl publicly accessible websites.
* Clarified why high volumes of crawler traffic can appear in website statistics without indicating a security breach.
* Compared the reporting capabilities of WordPress.com Stats with Google Analytics, explaining when more detailed traffic analysis would be beneficial.
* Recommended Google Analytics as a complementary tool for deeper visitor and traffic source reporting.
* Reassured the customer that the observed traffic did not, by itself, indicate that the website had been compromised.

#### **Result**

The customer gained confidence that the unusual international traffic reflected expected crawler activity rather than a security incident. By understanding how website statistics should be interpreted and when to use more advanced analytics tools, they were better equipped to monitor their site's traffic without unnecessary concern and had a clearer strategy for future analysis.

* Customer success and retention
* Customer reassurance
* Analytics interpretation
* Technical communication
* Security awareness
* Customer education
* Proactive guidance
