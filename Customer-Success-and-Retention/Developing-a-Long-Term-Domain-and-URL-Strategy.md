## **Developing a Long-Term Domain and URL Strategy**

#### **Situation**

A customer wanted to understand what would happen to hundreds of internal website links if they eventually stopped renewing their custom domain. They were concerned about the long-term impact on website functionality, SEO, and maintainability.

#### **Task**

Explain the implications of domain expiration, assess the potential impact on the customer's website and search visibility, and provide a long-term strategy that would minimize future disruption.

#### **Actions**

* Reviewed how WordPress.com handles expired custom domains and the resulting URL behavior.
* Explained what happens to a website and its links when a custom domain is no longer renewed.
* Clarified how absolute URLs would behave if the custom domain expired.
* Recommended using relative URLs for future internal links where appropriate to reduce dependency on a specific domain.
* Discussed the SEO implications of allowing a custom domain to expire.
* Suggested multi-year domain renewals as a long-term strategy for maintaining website stability and preserving search engine visibility.
* Presented multiple long-term options, allowing the customer to choose an approach that best aligned with their future plans.

#### **Result**

The customer gained a clear understanding of the long-term implications of domain ownership and internal URL management. Rather than receiving a single workaround, they left with a future-focused strategy that balanced SEO, maintainability, and business continuity, enabling them to make informed decisions about their website's long-term growth.
