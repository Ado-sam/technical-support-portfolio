## **Driving Customer Adoption Through Guided Onboarding**

#### **Situation**

A Premium customer became frustrated while trying to create their first blog post and began considering abandoning WordPress.com in favor of Blogger. The customer's difficulties stemmed from navigating an unfamiliar editing experience rather than a technical problem.

#### **Task**

Help the customer successfully publish their first content, reduce frustration with the platform, and build confidence using WordPress.com without overwhelming them with unnecessary information.

#### **Actions**

* Determined that the issue was related to usability and onboarding rather than a platform defect.
* Focused the conversation on the customer's immediate goal of creating and publishing their first post.
* Shared direct navigation links to the publishing interface to simplify access.
* Explained the difference between posts and pages so the customer could choose the appropriate content type.
* Introduced the Classic Block as an alternative editing option to better match the customer's expectations.
* Continued answering follow-up questions incrementally, introducing additional features only as they became relevant rather than providing excessive information at once.
* Maintained a supportive, goal-oriented approach throughout the interaction to build the customer's confidence using the platform.

#### **Result**

The customer's experience shifted from frustration and an intention to leave WordPress.com to actively exploring the platform's features. By simplifying the onboarding experience and providing guidance tailored to the customer's immediate needs, the interaction increased product confidence and engagement, with the customer commenting that WordPress.com now had their attention.

#### **Skills Demonstrated**

* Customer success and retention
* Customer de-escalation
* Product education
* Incremental onboarding
* Customer guidance
* Technical communication
* User adoption
* Relationship building
