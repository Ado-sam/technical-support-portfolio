## **Preventing an Unnecessary Account Cancellation Through Customer Discovery**

#### **Situation**

A customer who had previously experienced payment authorization issues later requested to reverse their Premium plan purchase and delete their WordPress.com account. Although the request appeared straightforward, deleting the account would also permanently remove multiple websites owned by the customer.

#### **Task**

Understand the customer's underlying objective before carrying out an irreversible request, clarify the consequences of account deletion, and help the customer choose the least destructive solution.

#### **Actions**

* Reviewed the customer's previous support history to understand the events leading to the cancellation request.
* Recognized that the customer had already invested significant effort attempting to purchase and use the Premium plan.
* Asked discovery questions to understand the customer's actual goal before processing the request.
* Explained the differences between:

  * Canceling a hosting plan.
  * Deleting an individual website.
  * Permanently deleting an entire WordPress.com account.
* Clearly outlined the consequences of permanent account deletion, including the removal of all associated websites.
* Presented alternative options that aligned with the customer's goals while avoiding unnecessary data loss.
* Focused the conversation on identifying the least disruptive solution before taking irreversible action.

#### **Result**

The interaction shifted from immediately processing an account deletion request to understanding the customer's underlying objective. By clarifying the available options and the consequences of each, the customer was able to consider less destructive alternatives, avoiding the unnecessary deletion of multiple websites and enabling a solution better aligned with their needs.
