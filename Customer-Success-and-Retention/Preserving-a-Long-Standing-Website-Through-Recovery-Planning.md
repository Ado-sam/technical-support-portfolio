## **Preserving a Long-Standing Website Through Recovery Planning**

#### **Situation**

A customer transferred their long-held domain from GoDaddy to WordPress.com and believed that their website—containing nearly 20 years of content—had disappeared. They no longer had access to the email address associated with their original WordPress.com account and were unable to satisfy the requirements for account recovery. They were also unfamiliar with the relationship between domain registration, website hosting, and WordPress.com accounts.

#### **Task**

Determine whether the website content had been lost, explain why it was no longer accessible, maintain account security requirements, and provide practical recovery options to help the customer preserve their website despite the inability to recover the original account.

#### **Actions**

* Investigated the historical hosting records associated with the transferred domain.
* Determined that the website content was not stored on the customer's newly created WordPress.com site.
* Identified the original WordPress.com site associated with the customer's legacy account.
* Explained the distinction between domain registration, website hosting, and WordPress.com accounts to clarify why transferring the domain had not transferred the website content.
* Guided the customer through the available account recovery options while adhering to account verification requirements.
* Recognized that account recovery could not proceed because sufficient ownership verification was unavailable.
* Shifted the conversation from account recovery to practical content recovery options rather than ending the interaction without a path forward.
* Provided guidance for recovering backups from GoDaddy if available.
* Explained how to export website content if the previous hosting environment remained accessible.
* Provided instructions for importing recovered content into the customer's new WordPress.com site.
* Recommended using the Internet Archive as a last-resort method for recovering historical content if no backups were available.
* Offered to make the inaccessible legacy site private to help protect its content while recovery options were being explored.

#### **Result**

The customer understood that the domain transfer had not deleted their website and gained a clear understanding of where the content resided. Although the original account could not be recovered because ownership verification requirements were not met, the customer left with multiple actionable recovery paths, including recovering backups from GoDaddy, exporting content from the previous host, importing it into the new WordPress.com site, and using archived copies if necessary. Instead of ending with an unresolved account recovery request, the interaction concluded with a structured preservation and recovery plan that enabled the customer to continue working toward restoring nearly two decades of website content.
