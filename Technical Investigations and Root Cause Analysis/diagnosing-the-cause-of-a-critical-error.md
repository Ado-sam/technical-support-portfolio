# **Diagnosing The Cause Of A Critical Error**

## **Situation**
A customer contacted WordPress.com after encountering a critical website error, believing their site was hosted on the WordPress.com platform.

## **Task**
Determine whether the issue fell within WordPress.com's support scope, identify the website's actual hosting environment, and direct the customer to the appropriate support path without unnecessary troubleshooting.

## **Actions**

* Reviewed the customer's account and website details to verify ownership and hosting information.
* Confirmed that the website was hosted with Bluehost rather than WordPress.com.
* Determined that the reported critical error originated within a self-hosted WordPress installation.
* Explained the distinction between WordPress.com-hosted sites and self-hosted WordPress websites.
* Clarified that plugins, themes, or server-side issues within the hosting environment commonly cause critical errors of this nature.
* Advised the customer to contact their hosting provider, who would have access to the necessary server logs and recovery tools to investigate the failure.

## **Result**
The investigation quickly established that the issue was outside WordPress.com's hosting environment, preventing unnecessary platform troubleshooting. The customer gained a clear understanding of the hosting distinction and was directed to the appropriate support team equipped to diagnose and resolve the critical error.

## **Skills Demonstrated**
* Hosting diagnosis
* Scope management
* Customer education
