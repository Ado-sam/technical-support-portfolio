## **Diagnosing Website Ownership on Amazon AWS**

#### **Situation**
A customer believed WordPress.com hosted both their domain and website after leaving a third-party website provider (Thriv). Although they had regained access to their domain through WordPress.com, they were unable to recover the website itself and assumed the website content should also be available.

#### **Task**
Determine where the website content was actually hosted, explain the distinction between domain registration and website hosting, and provide the customer with the correct recovery path.

#### **Actions**
* Investigated the website's domain registration and hosting configuration.
* Confirmed that the domain was registered through WordPress.com but the website itself was hosted on Amazon AWS.
* Explained the difference between domain registration and website hosting to clarify why recovering the domain did not restore the website content.
* Recommended using HostingChecker to independently verify the website's hosting provider.
* Clarified why WordPress.com could not recover website files hosted on AWS.
* Advised the customer to contact their previous provider to request access to the hosting account or obtain a backup of the website.
* Offered guidance on importing the recovered website into WordPress.com once the website files had been obtained.

#### **Result**
The investigation resolved the customer's confusion about the missing website by identifying external hosting as the source of the issue. The customer left with a clear recovery path: obtain hosting access or a website backup from the previous provider before migrating the content to WordPress.com.

#### **Skills Demonstrated**
* DNS and hosting diagnosis
* Domain vs. hosting education
* Third-party migration support
* Clear technical communication
