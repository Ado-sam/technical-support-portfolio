## **Diagnosing a Third-Party Email Delivery Issue**

#### **Situation**

A long-time subscriber reported that they could no longer reply to WordPress.com blog notification emails when using Yahoo Mail. The same reply functionality worked normally through ProtonMail, leading the customer to suspect there was a problem with WordPress.com's email service.

#### **Task**

Determine whether the reply failure originated within WordPress.com's email infrastructure or a third-party email provider, identify the true source of the issue, and provide accurate guidance without recommending unnecessary changes to the customer's website or account.

#### **Actions**

* Reviewed the customer's previous troubleshooting history to avoid repeating completed diagnostic steps.
* Compared email reply behavior across multiple email providers to identify whether the issue was platform-specific.
* Confirmed that replies worked through ProtonMail but consistently failed through Yahoo Mail.
* Used the cross-provider comparison to rule out WordPress.com's email infrastructure as the source of the problem.
* Explained why the successful behavior on other email providers indicated that WordPress.com's services were operating normally.
* Advised the customer to continue engaging with Yahoo regarding the issue, as the evidence pointed to a problem within Yahoo's email service.
* Managed customer expectations by explaining the limitations of resolving issues caused by third-party service providers.

#### **Result**

The investigation correctly identified a widespread Yahoo Mail outage as the root cause of the reply failures, eliminating unnecessary troubleshooting within WordPress.com. Yahoo later resolved the issue, confirming the diagnosis. The customer gained a clear understanding of the dependency on a third-party email provider, and no unnecessary website or account changes were made.

#### **Skills Demonstrated**
* Cross-platform troubleshooting
* Root cause analysis
* Third-party diagnosis
* Customer communication
