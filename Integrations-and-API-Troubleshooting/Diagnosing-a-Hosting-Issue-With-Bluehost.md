## Diagnosing a Hosting Issue With Bluehost

#### Situation
A customer reported that their website displayed a "There has been a critical error on this website" message and they could no longer access the WordPress admin dashboard (wp-admin). The customer believed the website was hosted on WordPress.com and expected the issue to be resolved through WordPress.com support.

#### Task
Determine whether the issue fell within WordPress.com's support scope, identify the website's actual hosting environment, and direct the customer to the appropriate support provider capable of resolving the critical error.

#### Actions
* Reviewed the customer's account information and verified that no WordPress.com account was associated with the reported website.
* Investigated the website's hosting environment to determine where it was hosted.
* Confirmed that the website was hosted by Bluehost as a self-hosted WordPress installation.
* Explained the distinction between WordPress.com-hosted websites and self-hosted WordPress installations.
* Clarified that WordPress.com could not diagnose or repair server-side plugin, theme, or hosting-related failures on externally hosted websites.
* Directed the customer to Bluehost with the appropriate context, explaining the likely causes of the critical error so the hosting provider could continue the investigation.

#### Result
The investigation established that the critical error originated outside the WordPress.com hosting environment, preventing unnecessary troubleshooting within the wrong support channel. The customer received an accurate diagnosis and was redirected to the hosting provider with the tools and access required to restore the website, avoiding further delays and confusion.
