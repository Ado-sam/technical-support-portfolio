## **Triaging Ecommerce Order Confirmation Issues on Third-Party Hosting**

#### **Situation**

A WooCommerce store owner reported that customers were no longer receiving order confirmation emails and that some customers were unable to complete purchases. The website was hosted with GoDaddy rather than WordPress.com, requiring an assessment of whether the issue fell within WordPress.com's support scope.

#### **Task**

Determine ownership boundaries, identify the appropriate troubleshooting path, and provide actionable diagnostics without unnecessarily escalating or redirecting the customer.

#### **Actions**

* Verified that the website was hosted by GoDaddy rather than WordPress.com.
* Determined that the issue originated outside WordPress.com's hosting environment.
* Avoided immediately referring the customer to another provider without first providing diagnostic guidance.
* Recommended reviewing the WooCommerce email settings to verify outbound email configuration.
* Advised checking the WooCommerce logs for email and checkout-related errors.
* Recommended creating a manual order to test both checkout functionality and order confirmation email delivery.
* Shared the relevant WooCommerce documentation to support the troubleshooting process.
* Asked the customer to return with the test results so the next stage of the investigation could be based on verified findings rather than assumptions.

#### **Result**

Rather than immediately redirecting the customer, the investigation provided a structured diagnostic workflow that could isolate whether the issue originated from WooCommerce configuration, checkout behavior, or the external hosting environment. This ensured the next support interaction would begin with concrete diagnostic evidence, reducing unnecessary troubleshooting and improving the efficiency of any subsequent investigation.

#### **Skills Demonstrated**

* Third-party troubleshooting
* Scope management
* Technical investigation
* Customer guidance
* Efficient triage
