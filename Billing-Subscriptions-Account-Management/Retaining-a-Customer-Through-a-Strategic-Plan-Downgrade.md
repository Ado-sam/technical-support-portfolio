## **Retaining a Customer Through a Strategic Plan Downgrade**

#### **Situation**

An agency managing multiple client websites wanted to downgrade one client's website from the Business plan because the annual cost had become difficult to justify. The agency believed loyal customers were being treated unfairly because newer Premium plans included plugin support while existing customers appeared to require the Business plan for the same functionality. As a result, they were considering moving their client websites away from WordPress.com.

#### **Task**

Determine whether the customer's required functionality could be preserved after a downgrade, address concerns about pricing and feature availability, and identify a solution that reduced the risk of customer churn.

#### **Actions**

* Acknowledged the customer's concerns regarding the differences between legacy and newer WordPress.com plan structures.
* Explained the distinction between the legacy Business plan and the newer Premium plan offerings.
* Verified internally whether an approved downgrade path existed that would preserve plugin functionality.
* Confirmed that the customer's existing plugins, including Elementor, could continue functioning after the downgrade.
* Clearly explained which Business-only features would no longer be available, including SFTP, SSH, staging, Git, database access, and reduced storage capacity.
* Recommended minimizing operational risk by testing the downgrade process on two smaller client websites before applying it to larger production sites.
* Helped the agency develop a phased migration approach based on the outcome of the pilot websites.

#### **Result**

Rather than migrating their clients away from WordPress.com, the agency agreed to retain the platform and begin by downgrading two smaller client websites as pilot implementations. They postponed changes to their larger client sites until the results had been validated and began planning multi-year Premium subscriptions if the pilot proved successful. The conversation shifted from potential customer churn to a phased rollout across additional managed websites.

#### **Skills Demonstrated**

* Customer retention
* Escalation management
* Product expertise
* Pricing objection handling
* Trust building
