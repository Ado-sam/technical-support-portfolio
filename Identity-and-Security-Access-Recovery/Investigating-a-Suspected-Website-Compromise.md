### **Investigating a Suspected Website Compromise**

#### **Situation**

A representative of a nonprofit organization reported that their website may have been hacked after navigation links appeared to redirect visitors to unfamiliar websites. The customer was concerned that the site had been compromised and required immediate investigation.

#### **Task**

Determine whether the website had been compromised, independently verify the reported behavior, and provide evidence-based guidance while avoiding unnecessary escalation if no compromise could be confirmed.

#### **Actions**

* Independently reproduced the reported navigation behavior to verify the customer's observations.
* Reviewed the website's menu configuration to confirm that navigation links were correctly configured.
* Manually tested the site's navigation to determine whether any redirects occurred during normal use.
* Used external redirect verification tools to check for unexpected or malicious redirects.
* Compared the reported behavior with the results of independent testing and found no evidence of an active compromise.
* Requested screenshots and additional evidence from the customer to support further investigation if the issue reoccurred.
* Recommended reviewing website users, passwords, plugins, and themes as precautionary security measures to strengthen the site's overall security posture.

#### **Result**

The investigation found no evidence that the website had been compromised, and the reported redirects could not be reproduced. By independently verifying the issue before drawing conclusions, unnecessary escalation was avoided while the customer received practical security recommendations and reassurance. The investigation remained evidence-driven, with additional information requested should new evidence become available.

#### **Skills Demonstrated**

* Security incident triage
* Independent verification
* Technical investigation
* Customer reassurance
* Security best practices
