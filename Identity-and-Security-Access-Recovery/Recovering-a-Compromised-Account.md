## **Recovering a Compromised Account**

#### **Situation**

A customer lost access to their WordPress.com account after an attacker changed the account email address and enabled two-factor authentication (2FA), preventing the legitimate owner from signing in. Although the customer eventually regained access, they remained concerned that unauthorized access might still exist.

#### **Task**

Verify that the customer had fully regained ownership of the account, confirm that no unauthorized access remained, strengthen the account's security, and investigate an additional account the customer believed may also have been compromised.

#### **Actions**

* Reviewed the account history and previous recovery actions to understand the compromise and recovery process.
* Verified the customer's account ownership, current authentication status, and account details.
* Checked for active authenticated sessions to determine whether any unauthorized access remained.
* Reviewed billing and ownership information to confirm the account had not been altered beyond the initial compromise.
* Guided the customer through securely regaining access to two-factor authentication using one-time recovery codes.
* Confirmed that the account recovery was successful and verified that only a single authenticated session remained active.
* Recommended replacing the existing authentication method with an authenticator app to improve account security.
* Investigated the customer's continued security concerns and identified a second account that required further review for potential compromise.

#### **Result**

The customer's ownership of the account was fully restored, unauthorized access was eliminated, and billing and ownership information were verified. The customer received guidance to strengthen future account security through an authenticator app and gained reassurance that the recovered account was secure. The investigation also identified a second potentially compromised account for further security review.
