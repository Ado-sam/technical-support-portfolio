## **Supporting a Custom Domain Configuration Issue**

#### **Situation**
A customer purchased a WordPress.com plan and wanted to reconnect their custom domain after migrating their website from Bluehost. Although the domain appeared to be connected, the website continued loading from the temporary staging URL instead of the custom domain.

#### **Task**
Determine whether the issue was caused by DNS configuration or a platform-level setting, and guide the customer through the correct steps to ensure the website loaded from the custom domain.

#### **Actions**
* Reviewed the customer's DNS and domain registrar configuration.
* Verified that the WordPress.com nameservers were correctly configured.
* Confirmed that the required DNS **A** records and registrar settings were correct.
* Determined that the DNS configuration was functioning as expected and ruled out a networking or propagation issue.
* Identified that the custom domain had not been configured as the site's **Primary Domain**.
* Guided the customer through setting the custom domain as the Primary Domain so it would become the site's public address.
* Followed up after the original support conversation when the issue remained unresolved to ensure the customer had the information needed to complete the configuration.

#### **Result**
The investigation ruled out DNS-related causes and identified a platform configuration issue as the source of the problem. The customer received precise guidance to complete the remaining configuration step required for the website to load from the custom domain rather than the staging URL.

#### **Skills Demonstrated**
* DNS troubleshooting
* Domain configuration
* Root cause analysis
* Website migration support
* Technical investigation
* Customer follow-up
