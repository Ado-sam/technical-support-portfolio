# **Supporting a Third-Party Email Delivery Issue**

## **Situation**
A long-time subscriber reported that they could no longer reply to WordPress.com blog notification emails when using Yahoo Mail. However, the same reply functionality worked normally when using ProtonMail, leading the customer to believe there was an issue with WordPress.com.

## **Task**
Determine whether the reply failure originated within WordPress.com's email infrastructure or with a third-party email provider, and provide clear guidance without recommending unnecessary changes to the customer's website or settings.

## **Actions**
* Reviewed the customer's previous troubleshooting history to avoid repeating completed diagnostic steps.
* Compared the email behavior across multiple providers and confirmed that replies functioned correctly through ProtonMail but consistently failed through Yahoo Mail.
* Used the cross-provider comparison to rule out WordPress.com's email infrastructure as the source of the issue.
* Explained why the successful behavior on other email providers indicated that WordPress.com's services were functioning as expected.
* Advised the customer to continue engaging with Yahoo support, as the issue appeared to originate within Yahoo's email service.
* Managed expectations by explaining the limitations of troubleshooting third-party email provider outages from the WordPress.com side.

## **Result**
* The investigation correctly identified the issue as an external Yahoo Mail service problem rather than a WordPress.com fault. Yahoo later resolved the outage, confirming the diagnosis. 
* By accurately identifying the source of the issue, unnecessary website changes and further platform troubleshooting were avoided, and the customer gained a clear understanding of the dependency on a third-party email provider.

## **Skills Demonstrated**
* Cross-platform troubleshooting
* Root cause analysis
* Third-party diagnosis
* Customer communication
